CUSD Project Team

Description

As the team lead of the Cornell University Sustainable Design (CUSD) Currents engineering project team, I worked to develop a machine learning-based smart HVAC controller to minimize energy usage and costs with a group of 12 fellow students. I led the design and building of a full-size model office outfitted with insulation and ductwork to test the system. I created the design, specified materials and parts, and coordinated logistics such as purchasing, deliveries, and building permits. This has also entailed leading building sessions which include teaching other students how to construct the room.

I also worked with MATLAB and Simulink to create thermal models of the office and predict the room's response to changes of the HVAC system and the outside environment. With this model, we predicted the time required to heat/cool the room in order to turn the system on to prepare the room for usage while conserving energy and prevent running the unit unnecessarily. This model was integrated with a predictive algorithm that integrated professors' habits, calenders, occupancy sensors,and manual inputs to predict when the room would be used.
